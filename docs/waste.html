Waste¶

Waste is a Scope 3 Emission that accounts for garbage or refuse, sludge from a wastewater treatment plant, water supply treatment plant, or air pollution control facility and other discarded material, resulting from industrial, commercial, mining, and agricultural operations, and from community activities.

The formula for \(\text{CO}_2\; Equivalent\; Emissions\) is:

\[\text{CO}_2\; Equivalent\; Emissions_{ma, me} = EF_{ma, me} \cdot Weight\]

This equation is derived from Equation 3.2 from [IPCC2006V5CH3].

where \(ma\) is the waste material, \(me\) is the disposal method, \(EF_{ma, me}\) is the emission factor is the emission factor for that \(ma\) and \(me\), and \(Weight\) is the weight, mass, or amount of the \(ma\) that has been disposed via \(me\) during that reporting period. Note that in atomic6, the calculations are done directly to \(\text{CO}_2\) by using the IPCC’s AR4 GWP values as a conversion factor, while in the reference material the calculations are done directly to \(\text{CH}_4\), the input units are converted to a standard unit of short tons, and the short tons are converted into kilograms.

For waste, the \(\text{CO}_2\; Equivalent\; Emissions_{disposal\; method}\) in metric tons is calculated. The formula is:

\[\text{CO}_2\; Equivalent\; Emissions_{disposal\; method} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} Total\; Emissions_{disposal\; method}\]

This equation is derived from [IPCC2006V5CH3].

where \(Total\; Emissions_{disposal\; method}\) is the sum of all the \(\text{CO}_2\;\) emissions for that \(disposal\; method\) (recycled, landfilled, combusted, composted, anaerobically digested (dry digestate with curing), or anaerobically digested (wet digestate with curing)). Note that in atomic6 the final value of this calculation is converted into \(metric\; tons\).

IPCC2006V5CH3(1,2)

IPCC, 2006: 2006 IPCC Guidelines for National Greenhouse Gas Inventories, Volume 5, IPPU, Chapter 3, Solid Waste Disposal, pp. 9
